# Incident investigation: latency_spike

**Severity: SEV-2** (confidence high) — Under the stated rules, SEV-2 applies when a critical flow shows latency >4x baseline or error rate in the 1-25% range. Booking-service p95 latency rose from a 450.0 ms baseline to 3200.0 ms at 14:40:00 (and stayed 3150-3300 ms through 15:00:00), roughly 7x baseline, and booking-service error_rate_pct rose from a 0.3% baseline to 4.8% at 14:40:00 (staying 4.6-4.8% through 15:00:00), within the 1-25% band. Neither metric crosses into SEV-1 territory (error rate stayed under 25%), so SEV-2 is the correct level per the rules, not the monitoring tool's own 'critical' label on alert_001.

## Summary

Starting around 2026-06-01T14:25:00+00:00, booking-service began showing elevated latency and error rates on the POST /book flow, culminating in an alert at 2026-06-01T14:35:00+00:00 for p95 latency of 2100.0 ms against a 1000.0 ms threshold; latency and error rate continued climbing and had not recovered as of the latest observed point (2026-06-01T15:00:00+00:00, 3200.0 ms / 4.8%). Multiple booking attempts are logged as failing after exhausting retries, and dependent queue and database signals also deviated sharply from baseline over the same window.

- affected services: booking-service, payment-service, appointments-db, booking-queue
- customer impact: Users attempting to book appointments experience significantly slower responses (booking-service p95 latency up to ~3200-3300 ms vs a 450.0 ms baseline) and a portion of booking attempts fail outright, as shown by repeated 'Booking failed ... eligibility lookup exhausted retries (5 of 5)' errors (e.g. appointments 84163, 84170, 84181) and failed traces (trace_incident_01 at 3180ms, trace_incident_02 at 3320ms).
- window: 2026-06-01T14:05:00+00:00 → ongoing (alert.triggered_at (2026-06-01T14:35:00+00:00) minus 30m lookback; deviated series not recovered -> ongoing)

## Ranked hypotheses

### 1. Unbounded-rate retries on a bounded 5-attempt policy amplified load on payment-service, appointments-db, and booking-queue, turning a slow dependency into a saturating cascade — confidence: medium

The booking-service retry policy (up to 5 attempts, no observed backoff) is consistent with multiplying request volume against the already-slow eligibility path, contributing to appointments-db connection pool saturation and booking-queue backlog beyond what the initial slowdown alone would cause.

- rubric: 3 aligned signal(s), timing aligned, 1 conflict(s)
- supporting evidence: `evidence_b4b51d1a99`, `evidence_b9acc61257`, `evidence_32ce625a89`, `evidence_ee16cd756a`, `evidence_be9279de40`, `evidence_c370cc7630`, `evidence_fdd6f0b8c7`, `evidence_b4b7403d5e`, `evidence_2a11057bb9`, `evidence_afafb0d376`, `evidence_933bb415f0`, `evidence_778c34805c`, `evidence_5d6e1e09a2`, `evidence_1ccfdc05e4`, `evidence_88ad9e4ec6`
- conflicting evidence: `evidence_a152329cae`
- assumptions: The 5-attempt, no-backoff retry configuration was already in place before the incident (i.e., not itself altered by deploy_002).; Queue depth growth (12 -> 545 baseline to peak) is substantially driven by retry volume rather than an independent increase in incoming booking requests.
- recommended checks: Inspect the eligibility-lookup retry implementation/configuration to confirm attempt count, timeout, and presence or absence of backoff.; Correlate booking-queue depth timestamps against retry-attempt log timestamps to check whether queue growth tracks retry volume rather than raw request volume.; If available, review appointments-db connection pool and CPU metrics beyond 2026-06-01T15:00:00 to determine whether saturation continued to worsen after the last observed retry/failure events.

### 2. payment_enrichment deploy introduced a slow, blocking appointments-db eligibility query that propagated latency/errors through booking-service — confidence: low

Deploy deploy_002, which enabled the payment_enrichment feature flag on booking-service at 2026-06-01T14:20:00+00:00, is consistent with triggering a chain of slow eligibility_query calls to appointments-db that increased payment-service and booking-service latency and error rates, culminating in booking failures and DB/queue saturation.

- rubric: 5 aligned signal(s), timing aligned, 2 conflict(s)
- supporting evidence: `evidence_5b1f1c42df`, `evidence_ed290016e0`, `evidence_ae2511b7ca`, `evidence_cd95530611`, `evidence_9f1a637ef1`, `evidence_677e014c54`, `evidence_f3288394a4`, `evidence_775204fa49`, `evidence_681f4df1e4`, `evidence_56496da9dd`, `evidence_d2bdd47471`, `evidence_4b58b13d8f`, `evidence_092d6008e6`, `evidence_a4a0c1c491`, `evidence_843b622df8`, `evidence_9571a38781`, `evidence_5d20469f1b`, `evidence_b15cbe8979`
- conflicting evidence: `evidence_f235549b5f`, `evidence_a152329cae`
- assumptions: The payment eligibility enrichment call added by deploy_002 is synchronous/blocking within the booking-service request path (not directly confirmed in the input).; The feature flag was applied to all or most traffic starting at 14:20:00 rather than ramped gradually (per known gap).; No other undisclosed config or flag changes occurred in the window that could independently explain the eligibility_query slowdown.
- recommended checks: Inspect payment-service code/config to confirm whether the eligibility enrichment call introduced by payment_enrichment is synchronous or asynchronous relative to the booking flow.; Inspect the appointments-db query plan and any index/statistics changes for the eligibility_rules table referenced in the 2350ms slow query at 14:40:51.; Compare booking-service and payment-service p95 latency time series in the 5-10 minutes immediately before and after the 14:20:00 deploy timestamp to look for a step change.; Review the feature-flag rollout configuration for payment_enrichment to determine whether it was enabled for 100% of traffic at 14:20:00 or ramped.

### 3. Unrelated prior notifications-service deploy as a contributing factor (alternative, largely unsupported) — confidence: low

The notifications-service template-copy deploy (deploy_001) on 2026-05-30 is not well supported as a contributor to the booking-service incident, given its timing and lack of a topological path to booking-service.

- rubric: 1 aligned signal(s), timing misaligned, 4 conflict(s)
- supporting evidence: `evidence_92ba3d7931`, `evidence_2148f72c5a`
- conflicting evidence: `evidence_b8c0d046f5`, `evidence_e3327252de`, `evidence_c206b40fe3`, `evidence_6c4a036f97`
- assumptions: Notifications-service and booking-service sharing a downstream booking-queue dependency does not itself imply a causal calling relationship in the direction needed to affect booking-service.
- recommended checks: Inspect the service topology graph to confirm there is no indirect calling path (as opposed to shared downstream dependency) from notifications-service to booking-service.; Review notifications-service logs across the full incident window (14:05-15:00) beyond the single clean batch-send entry at 14:58:20 to confirm continued normal operation.

## Postmortem draft

### Booking flow latency and error spike following payment eligibility enrichment rollout

Starting around 2026-06-01T14:20:00+00:00, a deploy enabled a payment eligibility enrichment feature flag on the booking service. Within minutes, the payment side of the eligibility lookup began showing elevated latency, and shortly after, the booking flow's error rate and a downstream queue's depth began deviating from baseline (both first crossing anomaly thresholds at 14:25:00). Booking-service latency crossed its anomaly threshold at 14:30:00, and a bounded retry policy (up to 5 attempts) began engaging on slow eligibility lookups around 14:29:00, with the first hard timeout logged at 14:31:08. Appointments-db connection pool usage climbed from nearing saturation (82/100 at 14:30:27) to saturated (97/100 at 14:34:44). An alert triggered at 14:35:00 per the incident window definition. Booking failures began appearing as retries were exhausted, with confirmed failures for appointments 84163 (14:35:52), 84170 (14:38:09), and 84181 (14:45:17). A specific slow database query against the eligibility_rules table (2350ms) was logged at 14:40:51. Latency, error rate, database CPU, and queue depth all continued to worsen or plateau at elevated levels through 14:50:00, with booking-service p95 latency peaking at 3300ms and error rate peaking at 4.8%, and appointments-db CPU peaking at 93%. By the last observed point (15:00:00), booking-queue depth had grown to 545 messages from a 12-message baseline with no sign of plateauing, and none of the affected signals had recovered; the incident remained ongoing as of the last available data.

**Impact.** Users attempting to book appointments experienced substantially slower responses on the booking flow, with p95 latency rising from a 450ms baseline to a peak of 3300ms (7.33x baseline) at 14:50:00 and remaining around 3200ms through the last observed point. A portion of booking attempts failed outright: booking-service error_rate_pct rose from a 0.3% baseline to a peak of 4.8% (16x baseline) at 14:50:00, sustained in the 4.6-4.8% range through the end of the observed window, with specific failed bookings logged for appointments 84163, 84170, and 84181 after exhausting retries, and degraded traces (trace_incident_01 at 3180ms, trace_incident_02 at 3320ms) also failing. Downstream, appointments-db connection pool usage reached saturation (97/100) and CPU peaked at 93% (2.45x its 38% baseline), and booking-queue depth grew from a 12-message baseline to a peak of 545 messages (45.4x baseline) with continued growth rather than stabilization. The exact number or proportion of affected users/bookings beyond the cited samples is not established in the available data.

**Contributing factors**
- Likely (medium confidence): the booking-service retry policy, which permits up to 5 attempts on eligibility lookups with no backoff documented in the runbook, amplified request load against an already-slow eligibility path, contributing to appointments-db connection pool pressure and booking-queue backlog beyond what the initial slowdown alone would cause.
- Possibly (low confidence): the payment_enrichment feature flag, enabled via deploy_002 at 2026-06-01T14:20:00+00:00, introduced a slow, blocking appointments-db eligibility query that triggered the initial latency and error increase; this is supported by tight timing and direct topological alignment, but the synchronous/blocking nature of the enrichment call and the scope of the flag rollout are not confirmed in the available evidence.

**Open questions**
- Whether booking-queue depth growth is substantially driven by retry volume or by an independent increase in incoming booking requests (queue-depth vs retry-timestamp correlation not yet established).
- Whether the payment_enrichment feature flag was enabled for 100% of traffic at 14:20:00 or ramped gradually, which affects how quickly impact could have appeared.
- What specifically inside the appointments-db eligibility_query (e.g., lock waits, connection waits, missing index) is consuming the observed 2150-2200ms, since no sub-spans below that query are available.
- Whether appointments-db CPU crossed and sustained above the runbook's 90%-for-15-minutes escalation threshold, given only a single 84% reading is confirmed in the provided window.
- Whether the observed conditions (connection pool saturation, queue backlog, elevated latency/error rate) worsened, stabilized, or recovered after the last provided data points (15:00:00 for metrics, 14:58:20 for logs).
- Whether other services depending on appointments-db or payment-service (e.g., stripe-api) were affected, since no metrics or logs for stripe-api are included.
- The exact scale of user impact (total booking request volume and proportion of distinct users affected) beyond the cited error-rate and failure-log samples.

**Action items**
- Inspect the eligibility-lookup retry implementation/configuration to confirm attempt count, timeout, and presence or absence of backoff, and consider adding backoff if absent.
- Correlate booking-queue depth timestamps against retry-attempt log timestamps to determine whether queue growth tracks retry volume rather than raw request volume.
