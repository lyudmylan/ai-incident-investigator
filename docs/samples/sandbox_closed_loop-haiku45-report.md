# Incident investigation: sandbox3-incident

**Severity: SEV-2** (confidence high) — Error rate 7.66%, inside the 1-25% SEV-2 band; p95 latency 1871.8 ms, approximately 20.7x baseline (90.5 ms), well above the 4x threshold for degradation on a critical flow. Checkout is a user-facing payment flow. Metrics first deviated at 2026-07-19T13:14:17+00:00 and remain elevated through 2026-07-19T13:22:17+00:00, indicating sustained degradation without recovery.

## Summary

Payment eligibility lookups in the checkout service are timing out consistently at 2000ms, causing checkout attempts to fail or exhaust retries. Error rate and latency have been elevated and stable for over 8 minutes without recovery.

- affected services: checkout-service
- customer impact: Users attempting to complete purchases experience checkout failures or long waits as the eligibility lookup retries multiple times before failing. Approximately 7.66% of checkout requests are failing.
- window: 2026-07-19T13:12:32+00:00 → ongoing (alert.triggered_at (2026-07-19T13:17:32+00:00) minus 5m lookback; deviated series not recovered -> ongoing)

## Ranked hypotheses

### 1. Eligibility Lookup Timeout Cascade via checkout_enrichment Feature — confidence: medium

The `checkout_enrichment` feature introduced a synchronous eligibility lookup call on the checkout path with a 5-attempt retry policy and no backoff. When the upstream eligibility/payment-gateway service became slow or unavailable, timeouts at 2000ms triggered retry amplification. Each order experienced 5 retry attempts spaced ~2 seconds apart (one timeout + overhead per attempt), causing ~20% of orders to exhaust retries and fail completely. This explains the coupled elevation of p95 latency (23.2x baseline) and error rate (19.3x baseline) beginning at 2026-07-19T13:14:17+00:00, with no recovery over 8+ minutes.

- conflicting evidence: `evidence_8e3060f1cc`

### 2. Upstream Dependency (Payment-Gateway or Orders-DB) Degradation or Timeout — confidence: medium

An upstream service dependency (payment-gateway or orders-db) that the eligibility lookup calls experienced degradation, latency spike, or partial outage beginning ~2026-07-19T13:14:00+00:00. The checkout-service's 2000ms synchronous timeout on this dependency caused consistent timeouts, triggering the retry amplification cascade. Without backoff between retries and with a 5-attempt hard limit, orders rapidly fail. The upstream service did not recover within the 8+ minute observation window, explaining sustained elevated latency and error rate.

### 3. Race Condition or Asynchronous Error Handling in Checkout Logging — confidence: low

Checkout-service exhibits contradictory success/failure signals in the same log entry: ERROR 'Eligibility lookup timed out' and WARN 'Retrying eligibility lookup' appear alongside INFO 'checkout ok' with reported latency_ms, despite orders subsequently failing with 'eligibility lookup exhausted retries'. This suggests a race condition or asynchronous error-handling flow where timeout errors are not preventing the checkout completion log from being written. However, this does not fully explain the metric deviations; elevated error rate and latency are still grounded in the real timeouts and failed orders (evidence_46a1968eed). This hypothesis describes a logging artifact that masks the true failure state rather than being the primary cause of elevated metrics.

- rubric: 1 aligned signal(s), timing aligned, 2 conflict(s)
- supporting evidence: `evidence_8e3060f1cc`, `evidence_2f459872a0`, `evidence_46a1968eed`
- conflicting evidence: `evidence_3d295bdc15`, `evidence_ad363324b3`
- assumptions: The 'checkout ok' log entry is written asynchronously or after the eligibility lookup attempt, allowing both success and failure signals to coexist in the same timestamp.; The contradictory logging is a symptom of the retry/timeout mechanism rather than the root cause of the incident.; The metric error_rate_pct is correctly calculated from actual order failures (evidenced by 'eligibility lookup exhausted retries' errors), not from log parsing artifacts.
- recommended checks: Inspect checkout-service source code for the order completion and error-handling flow to identify where 'checkout ok' is logged relative to eligibility lookup completion.; Verify whether error_rate_pct metric is derived from 'checkout ok' log entries or from explicit error/failure log entries (evidence_46a1968eed).; Compare log timestamps to confirm whether INFO 'checkout ok' and ERROR 'Eligibility lookup timed out' are written in the same transaction or separate async operations.; Review checkout-service error-handling and retry logic to determine if timeouts are caught, logged, and then retried without preventing the success log.
